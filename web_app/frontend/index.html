Soil Monitoring Features

Comprehensive soil analysis for optimal plant growth

Nitrogen (N)

Essential for plant growth, particularly leaf and stem development. Deficiency leads to yellowing leaves and stunted growth.

Phosphorus (P)

Crucial for root development, flowering, and fruit production. Deficiency can cause poor root growth and reduced yields.

Potassium (K)

Important for overall plant health, including strong stems, disease resistance, and water regulation.

pH Level

Measures soil acidity or alkalinity. Most plants thrive in a slightly acidic to neutral range (pH 6-7).

Humidity

Humidity influences plant transpiration, nutrient uptake, and disease risk. Ideal levels depend on crop type and growth stage.

Temperature

Soil temperature affects seed germination, root growth, and microbial activity. Optimal ranges vary by species.
